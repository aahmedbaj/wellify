Daily Calorie Needs

How much energy does your body need? Use this guide to get a quick estimate and smart tips for fueling your day.

What are calories?

Calories measure the energy in food. Your daily calorie needs depend on age, sex, and activity level.
Consume more than you use to gain weight, less than you use to lose basic but true.

Quick Reference: Recommended Daily Calories

These are general ranges for adults. Use them as a starting point - not gospel.

Group Sedentary Moderately Active Active
Men (18-30) 2,400 kcal 2,600-2,800 kcal 3,000 kcal
Women (18-30) 1,800 kcal 2,000-2,200 kcal 2,400 kcal
Men (31-50) 2,200 kcal 2,400-2,600 kcal 2,800-3,000 kcal
Women (31-50) 1,800 kcal 2,000 kcal 2,200 kcal

Note: "Moderately active" means moderate exercise 3-5 days/week.

Practical Tips

Balance, not ban

Focus on nutrient-dense foods: whole grains, lean proteins, healthy fats, and lots of veggies.

Portions > Perfection

Small changes (like a smaller plate) can reduce daily calories without feeling hungry.

Move a little more

Extra walking or stair-climbing adds up - and lets you enjoy a treat now and then.

FAQ
Can I use these numbers to plan meals?

Yes - they help estimate total daily energy. For meal planning, split calories across meals/snacks (e.g., 3 meals + 1-2 snacks) and focus on balance.

Are calories the only thing that matters?

Nope. Quality matters too - vitamins, minerals, fiber, and protein affect health beyond raw calorie counts.
